# Safe Rodent Cleanup: A Complete Guide

Cleaning up after rodents isn't just about maintaining a clean home—it's about protecting your health. Rodent droppings, urine, and saliva can carry Hantavirus, a serious and potentially fatal respiratory disease. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the CDC-recommended procedures for safe rodent cleanup.

## Why Safe Cleanup Matters

Hantavirus becomes dangerous when dried rodent droppings, urine, or saliva are disturbed and become airborne. When you sweep or vacuum these materials without proper precautions, you can inhale virus particles, which can lead to Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS).

**The critical rule:** NEVER sweep or vacuum dry rodent droppings.

## Essential Supplies

Before beginning any cleanup, gather these supplies:

### Required Items
- Rubber or latex gloves
- N95 respirator mask (or higher protection level)
- Spray bottle
- Household bleach
- Water
- Paper towels
- Mop and bucket
- Heavy-duty plastic bags

### Recommended Items
- Protective eyewear
- Disposable coveralls
- Shoe covers
- Long-sleeved clothing
- Additional disinfectant
- Sponge mop

## Step-by-Step Cleanup Procedure

### Step 1: Ventilate the Area

Open all windows and doors in the contaminated area for at least 30 minutes before cleaning. This allows fresh air to circulate and reduces virus concentration in the air.

**Tips:**
- Leave the area during ventilation if possible
- Use fans to increase airflow if available
- Keep the area ventilated throughout the cleaning process

### Step 2: Put on Protective Equipment

Wear rubber or latex gloves and an N95 respirator mask. Ensure the mask fits properly and forms a seal around your face.

**Safety note:** The mask should be the last thing you put on and the first thing you remove after cleaning.

### Step 3: Prepare Disinfectant Solution

Mix a solution of 1 part bleach to 10 parts water (approximately 1½ cups of bleach per gallon of water). Fill a spray bottle with the solution.

**Alternative:** Use a commercial disinfectant registered as effective against Hantavirus.

### Step 4: Wet the Contaminated Area

Thoroughly spray the contaminated area (droppings, urine, nesting materials) with the disinfectant solution. Ensure the area is completely wet.

**Important:** Let it soak for at least 10 minutes to kill the virus before proceeding.

### Step 5: Clean with Wet Materials

Using paper towels or a damp mop, wipe up the wet droppings and nesting materials. Place all contaminated materials in a plastic bag.

**Remember:** Never use dry sweeping or vacuuming at any point.

### Step 6: Double-Bag the Waste

Seal the plastic bag containing the contaminated materials tightly. Place this bag inside a second plastic bag and seal it as well.

This double-bagging prevents any virus from escaping during disposal.

### Step 7: Disinfect the Area Again

Spray the cleaned area once more with the disinfectant solution. Mop floors or wipe surfaces with the disinfectant to ensure any remaining virus is killed.

### Step 8: Dispose of Waste Properly

Place the double-bagged waste in your regular trash for disposal. Do not compost or recycle contaminated materials.

### Step 9: Remove and Clean Protective Gear

Carefully remove gloves by turning them inside out as you take them off. Dispose of disposable gloves in the trash. If using reusable gloves, wash them with soap and hot water.

Remove the mask last, being careful not to touch the front of the mask.

### Step 10: Wash Hands Thoroughly

Wash your hands with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds. Use hand sanitizer if soap and water are not available.

## Special Situations

### Cleaning Cabins or Sheds

Buildings that have been closed for extended periods require extra precautions:

- Open all doors and windows for at least 30 minutes before entering
- Wear full protective equipment (gloves, mask, eyewear, coveralls)
- Leave the area if you see heavy rodent infestation
- Consider professional cleaning for severe infestations
- Air out the building for several hours after cleaning

### Cleaning Vehicles

If rodents have been in vehicles:

- Open all doors and windows before cleaning
- Remove and dispose of any nesting materials
- Vacuum only after disinfecting (use HEPA filter if possible)
- Wipe all surfaces with disinfectant
- Steam clean upholstery if contaminated

### Cleaning Attics and Crawl Spaces

For confined spaces with poor ventilation:

- Ensure adequate ventilation before entering
- Use a higher-grade respirator (P100 if available)
- Work in short shifts to avoid prolonged exposure
- Have someone nearby in case of emergency
- Consider professional cleaning for large areas

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Never sweep dry droppings
- ❌ Never vacuum without disinfecting first
- ❌ Never touch droppings with bare hands
- ❌ Never clean without ventilation
- ❌ Never skip the disinfectant
- ❌ Never touch your face while cleaning

## Cleaning Contaminated Clothing or Bedding

If clothing or bedding has been contaminated:

1. Wear gloves and handle contaminated items carefully
2. Do not shake the items
3. Wash in hot water (at least 140°F/60°C) with laundry detergent
4. Use bleach if the fabric allows
5. Dry on high heat setting if possible
6. Wash hands thoroughly after handling

## After Cleaning

Monitor your health for symptoms after cleaning rodent-contaminated areas:

- Watch for symptoms for up to 8 weeks after exposure
- Early symptoms include fever, muscle aches, fatigue, headaches
- Seek medical care immediately if breathing difficulties develop
- Inform your healthcare provider about potential rodent exposure
- Keep a record of when and where the exposure occurred

## Professional Cleaning

Consider hiring professional cleaning services if:

- The contaminated area is large
- There is heavy rodent infestation
- You have health conditions that increase risk
- You're unsure about proper procedures
- The area is difficult to access or ventilate

## Prevention is Better Than Cleanup

The best way to deal with rodent contamination is to prevent it:

- Seal entry points to prevent rodents from entering
- Store food in rodent-proof containers
- Remove clutter and potential nesting sites
- Use traps for rodent control
- Conduct regular inspections for signs of rodents

Safe rodent cleanup is a critical skill for anyone who may encounter rodent contamination. By following these CDC-recommended procedures, you can protect yourself and your family from Hantavirus and other rodent-borne diseases.
