# Hantavirus Emergency Guide: What to Do in a Crisis

Knowing what to do in a Hantavirus emergency can save lives. This guide provides immediate action steps for suspected exposure, developing symptoms, or medical emergencies related to Hantavirus. Print this guide and keep it accessible.

## Immediate Exposure Response

### Just Had Exposure?

**If You Were Just Exposed to Rodent Droppings:**

1. **Leave the area immediately**
   - Don't continue cleaning
   - Get fresh air
   - Don't disturb contaminated materials further

2. **Remove contaminated clothing**
   - Remove outer clothing carefully
   - Place in plastic bag
   - Wash separately in hot water
   - Shower thoroughly

3. **Wash thoroughly**
   - Wash hands with soap and warm water for 20+ seconds
   - Wash face and any exposed skin
   - Don't touch your face during washing
   - Use hand sanitizer if soap unavailable

4. **Monitor yourself**
   - Watch for symptoms for 8 weeks
   - Take temperature if you feel ill
   - Keep a record of exposure date
   - Note any symptoms that develop

5. **Don't panic**
   - Not every exposure leads to infection
   - Most exposures don't result in illness
   - Stay calm and monitor your health
   - Seek care only if symptoms develop

### Large Scale Exposure

**If Multiple People Were Exposed:**
- Ensure everyone leaves the area
- Document who was exposed
- Note the date and circumstances
- Everyone should monitor for symptoms
- Consider informing local health department

## Recognizing Emergency Symptoms

### Early Warning Signs (Seek Medical Care)

**Go to Urgent Care or See Doctor If:**
- Fever above 101°F (38.3°C)
- Severe muscle aches
- Extreme fatigue
- Severe headaches
- Dizziness
- These symptoms AND potential rodent exposure

**What to Tell the Doctor:**
- "I may have been exposed to Hantavirus"
- Describe the exposure (when, where, how)
- Mention your symptoms
- Ask if Hantavirus testing is appropriate
- Be persistent if you're concerned

### Emergency Symptoms (Call 911 or Go to ER)

**Call 911 Immediately If:**
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Chest pain or pressure
- Coughing with fluid
- Bluish lips or skin
- Confusion or severe dizziness
- Fainting or loss of consciousness

**While Waiting for Emergency Help:**
- Stay calm and try to rest
- Sit upright to help breathing
- Loosen tight clothing
- Have someone stay with you
- Unlock doors for emergency responders
- Gather your medications and medical information

## Medical Emergency Preparedness

### Information to Have Ready

**Before an Emergency, Prepare:**
- List of current medications
- Medical history
- Allergies
- Emergency contact information
- Insurance information

**For Hantavirus Specifically:**
- Record of any rodent exposure
- Dates and locations of exposure
- Timeline of symptom development
- Contact information for your regular doctor

### Emergency Contacts

**Keep These Numbers Available:**
- 911 or local emergency number
- Poison Control (1-800-222-1222)
- Your doctor's office
- Nearest hospital emergency room
- Local health department

## At the Hospital

### What to Expect

**Emergency Department:**
- Triage assessment upon arrival
- Vital signs monitoring
- Oxygen level check
- Blood tests
- Chest X-ray
- Possible admission

**Communicate Clearly:**
- "I may have been exposed to Hantavirus"
- Describe the exposure in detail
- Explain your symptoms
- Mention when symptoms started
- Provide exposure timeline

### Questions to Ask

**If Admitted:**
- "Do you have experience treating Hantavirus?"
- "Will I be transferred to a facility with ICU?"
- "What treatments will I receive?"
- "What is my prognosis?"
- "Can my family visit?"

**If Not Admitted:**
- "What symptoms should prompt return?"
- "When should I seek emergency care?"
- "What monitoring should I do at home?"
- "Can I get Hantavirus testing?"
- "What warning signs require immediate return?"

## Family Emergency Actions

### If Family Member is Sick

**Supportive Actions:**
- Help them get medical care
- Stay calm and reassuring
- Assist with communication
- Gather medications and information
- Contact other family members

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't delay seeking care
- Don't dismiss symptoms
- Don't wait to "see if it gets better"
- Don't rely on home remedies
- Don't ignore breathing difficulties

### If You're the Caregiver

**Taking Care of Yourself:**
- Take breaks when needed
- Get support from others
- Maintain your own health
- Ask questions of medical team
- Accept help from others

## Workplace Emergency

### On-the-Job Exposure

**Immediate Actions:**
- Stop work immediately
- Leave the contaminated area
- Report to supervisor
- Complete incident report
- Seek medical evaluation if symptomatic

**Documentation:**
- Document exposure details
- Get copy of incident report
- Record witness information
- Take photos if appropriate
- Keep all medical records

### Medical Care for Workers

**Worker's Compensation:**
- Report through employer
- Seek appropriate medical care
- Document all medical expenses
- Follow employer's procedures
- Know your rights

## Post-Emergency Recovery

### After Hospitalization

**Initial Recovery:**
- Follow all medical instructions
- Attend all follow-up appointments
- Take prescribed medications
- Rest and recover gradually
- Monitor for returning symptoms

**Home Recovery:**
- Arrange for help at home
- Prepare recovery space
- Stock up on medications and supplies
- Plan for limited activity initially
- Have emergency contacts available

### Long-Term Follow-Up

**Medical Monitoring:**
- Regular check-ups with healthcare provider
- Lung function tests if recommended
- Cardiac evaluation if needed
- Kidney function monitoring
- Mental health support if needed

### For Home

### For Travel

**Travel Emergency Kit:**

## Prevention of Future Emergencies

### Reduce Risk

**Long-Term Prevention:**
- Implement rodent control measures
- Follow safe cleaning procedures
- Educate family about risks
- Maintain clean living environment
- Regular inspections for rodents

### Emergency Planning

**Be Prepared:**
- Know location of nearest hospital
- Have emergency contact numbers
- Keep medical information accessible
- Know when to seek emergency care
- Have transportation plan

## Special Populations

### For Pregnant Women

**Special Considerations:**
- Seek medical care promptly for any symptoms
- Inform obstetrician about exposure
- May need specialized monitoring
- Treatment may need modification
- Fetal monitoring may be indicated

### For Elderly Individuals

**Special Considerations:**
- May have more severe illness
- May have other health conditions
- May need more aggressive treatment
- Recovery may take longer
- May need additional support

### For People with Chronic Conditions

**Special Considerations:**
- May be at higher risk for complications
- May need medication adjustments
- May need specialized care
- Should inform doctors of all conditions
- May need closer monitoring

## Communication Tips

### Talking to Healthcare Providers

**Be Clear and Specific:**
- "I may have been exposed to Hantavirus"
- Provide exposure details
- Describe symptoms clearly
- Give timeline of events
- Ask questions if unsure

**Be Persistent:**
- If symptoms are severe, insist on evaluation
- If concerned, ask for second opinion
- If condition worsens, return for care
- Advocate for yourself or your loved one

### Talking to Family

**Share Information:**
- Explain the situation clearly
- Provide accurate information
- Avoid speculation
- Focus on facts
- Keep communication open

## Legal and Administrative

### Documentation

**Keep Records Of:**
- All medical visits
- Test results
- Treatment received
- Expenses incurred
- Time off work

**For Occupational Exposure:**
- Incident reports
- Workers' compensation claims
- Medical evaluations
- Return-to-work documentation
- Communications with employer

## Bottom Line

Emergency preparedness for Hantavirus:

1. **Know the symptoms** - recognize when to seek care
2. **Act quickly** - don't delay medical care for breathing difficulties
3. **Communicate clearly** - tell medical providers about potential exposure
4. **Be prepared** - have emergency information ready
5. **Follow through** - complete all recommended follow-up care

In a Hantavirus emergency, time is critical. Early recognition and prompt medical care can save lives. Don't hesitate to seek emergency care if you or someone else shows severe symptoms, especially breathing difficulties.

Remember: it's always better to seek care and be told it's not Hantavirus than to delay care when it is. When in doubt, err on the side of caution and seek medical attention.
